Butterbeer

  • Time: 5 minutes
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Heavy whipping cream 2 cups
Sweetened condensed milk 14 oz
Sugar 2 tablespoon
Butterscotch topping 1/2 cup

Step 1: In a large bowl, beat the whipping cream until doubled in size and peaks have formed. (Don't over beat or the texture will be lumpy!)

Step 2: Gently fold in the sweetened condensed milk until totally combined.

Step 3: Pour the ice cream base into a pan or bowl with an air tight lid. Pour the butterscotch over the base and use a knife to swirl it around so there are ribbons throughout.

Step 4: Cover and freeze the ice cream for 6-8 hours, or until frozen through. Scoop the ice cream into mugs, bowls, or cones. Serve with extra butterscotch topping as desired and enjoy!

Exploding Bonbons

  • Time: Three times on your Time-turner will suffice (and if you don't possess one, well... Good luck)
  • Serves: Fred and George will eat them before you can
Ingredients Quantity
Cake, cookie or brownie mix 1 box
Cream cheese, room temperature 8 oz
Pop Rocks, varying colors 5 packets
Candy melts 1 cup
Crisco (per cup of candy melts) 1 tablespoon
Parchment paper

Step 1: Prepare the cake, cookies or brownies according to the ingredients and instructions on the box. Cook and cool completely before starting the bonbons. (From here on out, I will refer to brownies, since those were my base.)

Step 2: Crumble the brownies in a food processor (or by hand) until the crumbs are fine and smaller than a half inch in size. Pour the crumbs into a bowl with the room temperature cream cheese, and stir until a thick dough forms. Chill for ten minutes.

Step 3: Use the palms of your hands to form the dough into quarter sized balls. Arrange the brownie balls on a cookie sheet lined with parchment paper. Chill for thirty minutes to an hour.

Step 4: Before taking the brownie balls out of the fridge, melt the candy melts. Add a tablespoon of Crisco to each melted cup of candy melts.

Step 5: Use a fork to roll the brownie balls through the candy melts until completely coated. Place back onto the parchment lined cookie sheet.

Step 6: Sprinkle the Pop Rocks onto the tops of each wet brownie ball. Pop back into the fridge to chill for an additional ten minutes, or until the candy has hardened. Serve at your Harry Potter themed party. Enjoy!
